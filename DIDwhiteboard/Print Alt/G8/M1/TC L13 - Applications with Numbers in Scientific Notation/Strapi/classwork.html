1. On average, an eighth grader’s lungs can hold 3.6×10−3 cubic meters of air per breath.
    1. What volume of air is in 2 breaths? Write your answer in scientific notation.
      ( 3.6 × 10 − 3 ) + ( 3.6 × 10 − 3 ) = ( 3.6 + 3.6 ) × 10 − 3 = 7.2 × 10 − 3
      There are 7.2×10−3 cubic meters of air in 2 breaths.  

    2. What volume of air is in 10 breaths? Write your answer in scientific notation.
      10 ( 3.6 × 10 − 3 ) = 10 ( 3.6 ) × 10 − 3 = 36 × 10 − 3 = 3.6 × 10 − 2
      There are 3.6×10−2 cubic meters of air in 10 breaths.
